HERO NEEDED TO KILL THE MINER KING
Brave Volunteer Sought to Descend Into the Cursed Mine and End the Tyranny of the Purple Beast Below

TOWN IN PERIL

For seven long years the cursed mine outside our humble village has been ruled by a horrible monster known only as the MINER KING. No miner who has ventured into his domain has ever returned, and our once-rich tunnels lie silent and abandoned.

Gold, silver, ruby, emerald, diamond, sapphire, obsidian, mythril, titanium, uranium, plasma — all of it remains untouched in the deep layers, just waiting for a brave soul to claim it.

Local elders say the King first appeared after the great cave-in of '42. None know where he came from, but all agree: he must be stopped.

The town of Pebblebrook, once the proudest mining settlement in the kingdom, now languishes in poverty. The market square stands empty. The forge has gone cold. The famous Pickaxe Festival has been cancelled for the seventh year running. Children grow up never having seen a single ruby, much less held one.

THE LAYOUT, AS WE KNOW IT

Above ground stands SHOP STREET, where the kindly Shopkeeper sells pickaxes, healing tonics, water buckets, and hires loyal workers.

Below the surface is the rich ORE LAYER, with shallow dirt giving way to deeper, more valuable veins. Beware: streams of LAVA have been spotted in the deepest tunnels!

Further down lies the dreaded MAZE, a twisting labyrinth of stone passages. Get through it, and you reach THE CHAMBER — the throne room of the Miner King himself.

Beyond the chamber, the wise say, there are even deeper levels where the rarest ores glow with otherworldly light. None have ever returned to confirm this. But our scribes have collected dozens of fragmented maps, and they all point to the same conclusion: there is MORE BELOW.

A BRIEF HISTORY OF THE MINE

The mine was first dug in the year 1607 by the famous explorer Jebediah Dustpan. In its golden age (1742–1842) it produced more diamonds than any other mine in the known world. Three monarchs were crowned with jewels from these very tunnels.

Then came the Great Quake. Then came the King.

WEATHER & HAZARDS

Surface: Mild & sunny. Crowd expected on Victory Day.

Shallow Layer: Dry. Occasional dust clouds.

Mid Layer: Cooler. Rocks may fall. Wear your helmet.

Deep Layer: Cloudy with a chance of falling rocks. Lava advisory in effect below 1,200m.

The Chamber: Foreboding. Hostile. Bring bombs.

SPORTS

The annual ROCK-PUNCHING tournament was cancelled this year due to lack of rocks. The town's only undefeated puncher, "Granite" Greg, has retired to take up knitting.

Mrs. Henrietta Bellows hosted her famous Tuesday Tea Tuesday, though attendance was low. Several guests reportedly slipped away to look for the hero before dessert was served.
